Step 4e: Key Finding


We see a sharp drop off in active listings and new listings around December of each year. We see a peak of new listings

between March and June which translates into a peak Active listing count between July and September. This suggests that

the home buying season is from March to September. Some reason for the sharp drop in demand from October to February

could be:

1) Weather, it gets cold in Colorado

2) School year, parents may be waiting to move when kids are out of school

3) Difficulty for sellers to find a new home due to lack of inventory

4) The Holiday season has people spending time with family instead of real estate shopping

The number of days on the market peaks in December, suggesting that these are the homes that have been listed all

summer

and were not purchased. As a seller, there seems to be no advantage to listing their home when buyers don't have a

multitude

of homes to choose from. As such, sellers will need to more competitive with their prices and quality of home during the

summer when the buyers are more active.
